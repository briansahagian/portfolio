Fixed Gear Bicycle Build

Complete conversion of 1988 Panasonic road bike frame to fixed-gear configuration, including component sourcing, frame repair coordination, and custom assembly

Personal Project Individual Work Multi-week Build

Project Overview

This project involved the complete restoration and conversion of a vintage 1988 Panasonic road bike frame into a modern fixed-gear bicycle. The build required extensive component sourcing, frame preparation, professional welding coordination, and precise assembly to create a functional and aesthetically pleasing urban commuter bike.

The project demonstrated mechanical problem-solving, component compatibility analysis, and hands-on bicycle mechanics while preserving the vintage character of the original frame. The result is a reliable, efficient, and stylish fixed-gear bicycle optimized for urban cycling.

Build Specifications

  • Frame: 1988 Panasonic road bike frame (steel construction)
  • Drivetrain: Fixed gear conversion with horizontal dropouts
  • Wheels: Modern alloy wheelset with fixed hub
  • Components: Mix of vintage and modern parts for optimal performance
  • Finish: Powder coating and frame restoration

Build Process

  1. Frame Assessment: Complete inspection and damage evaluation
  2. Frame Preparation: Stripping, cleaning, and professional welding repairs
  3. Component Sourcing: Research and procurement of compatible parts
  4. Drivetrain Setup: Fixed gear conversion and chain line optimization
  5. Assembly & Adjustment: Complete bike assembly and fine-tuning
  6. Testing & Refinement: Road testing and final adjustments

Key Achievements

  • Successfully converted vintage road bike to modern fixed-gear configuration
  • Coordinated professional frame welding and repair services
  • Sourced and integrated compatible vintage and modern components
  • Achieved optimal chain line and drivetrain efficiency
  • Created reliable urban commuter bicycle with vintage aesthetic
  • Developed comprehensive understanding of bicycle mechanics
