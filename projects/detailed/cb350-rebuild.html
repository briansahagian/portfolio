1969 Honda CB350 Complete Rebuild

Full engine teardown, restoration, and rebuild of a classic motorcycle with comprehensive documentation

Personal Project Individual Work Summer Project

Project Overview

This 1969 Honda CB350 came to me as a non-running motorcycle in extremely poor condition. The engine was seized, components were heavily corroded, and decades of neglect had taken their toll. This became my first complete engine rebuild project, requiring meticulous attention to detail and precision work.

The project demanded not only mechanical skills but also patience, research, and the ability to work with limited documentation while maintaining the integrity of this classic motorcycle's design. Over the course of a full summer, I completely disassembled, restored, and rebuilt the entire engine.

Vehicle Specifications

  • Make & Model: 1969 Honda CB350
  • Engine Type: 325cc parallel twin, 4-stroke
  • Initial Condition: Non-running, seized engine
  • Project Scope: Complete engine teardown and rebuild
  • Timeline: Full summer project (3+ months)
  • Documentation: Comprehensive photo documentation of process

Restoration Process

  1. Complete Disassembly: Stripped engine to individual components with detailed documentation
  2. Cleaning & Assessment: Thorough cleaning and condition evaluation of all parts
  3. Parts Sourcing: Research and procurement of replacement components and gaskets
  4. Machine Work: Professional machine shop services for critical engine components
  5. Reassembly: Precise rebuilding following factory specifications and procedures
  6. Testing & Tuning: Initial startup, break-in, and performance optimization

Key Achievements

  • Successfully freed seized engine through careful disassembly techniques
  • Restored engine to running condition with smooth operation
  • Learned advanced mechanical repair and restoration techniques
  • Completed comprehensive documentation for future reference
  • Gained experience with vintage motorcycle engineering and design

Detailed Work Accomplished

Engine Teardown

The engine was in such poor condition that complete disassembly was necessary. I carefully documented the removal process, taking detailed photos and notes to ensure proper reassembly. Each component was individually assessed for wear, damage, and serviceability.

Component Restoration

Critical engine components required extensive cleaning and restoration. This included removing decades of carbon buildup, rust treatment, and careful inspection of wear surfaces. Components beyond repair were sourced and replaced with appropriate period-correct or modern equivalents.

Precision Reassembly

The rebuild process required meticulous attention to detail, ensuring proper torque specifications, clearances, and timing. Each step was verified against factory documentation, and the assembly was completed with the precision expected of professional engine work.
